Your personal library, beautifully organized.

TomeShelf helps you identify books quickly, keep your shelves organized, capture the right level of metadata for each copy, and build a library that stays useful across devices.

Features
📷
Identify One Book

Use a barcode, ISBN, or up to 3 photos of the cover, title page, and copyright page. TomeShelf can do a fast Quick Add or a more exact Specific Edition lookup.

📸
Bulk Load

Add books from a bookshelf photo, a batch of individual book photos, or a text list. Review matches before anything is added.

📖
Shelves

Organize your catalog into named shelves, move books in bulk, and keep separate collections without losing one searchable library.

🖉
Metadata Refresh

Already added a book? Re-run lookup from saved photos, new photos, ISBN, or manual search to improve title, author, edition, and year details without re-adding it.

🔎
Research Briefings

Rich background briefings are generated automatically for your books, with spoiler-safe handling for fiction and on-demand regeneration when needed.

🎧
Audio Briefings

Listen to a narrated version of any briefing on demand, with multiple voice options. Fiction titles can offer both spoiler-safe and spoiler-inclusive audio versions.

🖼
Covers and Photos

Keep your own cover photo, search online, replace covers from camera or upload, and save additional pages or condition photos with each record.

🔗
Share Your Shelves

Generate read-only links for shelves or individual books. Control whether notes, additional photos, Wikipedia lookup, and audio are included.

☁

Your catalog, photos, and briefings stay in sync across devices. Guest mode lets you try everything before linking to Google.

Everything TomeShelf can do.

Adding Books
  • Barcode scanning — scan ISBN barcodes with your phone camera; falls back to html5-qrcode on browsers without native BarcodeDetector support
  • Photo Book Lookup — identify one book from up to 3 images; best results come from cover, title page, and copyright/publisher page; users can mark which selected image is the cover
  • Quick Add vs. Specific Edition — Quick Add focuses on work-level metadata, while Specific Edition keeps edition detail evidence-first and can use page extraction plus Perplexity for non-ISBN photo flows
  • Alternate candidates — when multiple candidates are found, all are shown with cover thumbnails; selecting an alternate candidate swaps it to the top while keeping the previous pick accessible
  • Bulk Load: Bookshelf Photo — photograph a shelf or group of books; AI reads visible spines and covers; review a ✓/✗ list before anything is added, with year saved when evidence is strong enough
  • Bulk Load: Batch Photos — select up to 20 individual book photos at once; each image is treated as one book; alternate candidates and cover choices are shown per item, with fuller correction review when edition evidence is weak
  • Bulk Load: Titles in Text — extract books from a photographed list or pasted text and review them before adding
  • Search Results — cover thumbnails appear inline in search results; “Try Broader Search” relaxes query constraints when initial matches are poor; each candidate shows a plain-language match cue
  • Metadata Refresh — existing books can rerun lookup from saved photos, new photos, ISBN, or manual search without creating a duplicate record
Managing Your Library
  • Shelves — organize books into multiple named shelves; create, rename, and delete shelves; last-viewed shelf restored on next open
  • Multi-book selection — select any or all books on a shelf; move the selection to another shelf or delete in bulk with confirmation; deleted books can be immediately restored via an Undo button in the confirmation toast
  • Full catalog fields — title, author, year, publisher, edition, ISBN, subjects, cover art, additional photos, condition, shelf location, reading status, dates, rating, and notes
  • Search, sort & filter — search by any field; filter by reading status, condition, or shelf; sort by title, author, year, rating, or recently added
  • Duplicate detection — adding a book with the same title and author as an existing entry shows a warning with an “Add Anyway” option, allowing legitimate multiple editions
  • Book photos — save additional photos such as inscriptions, title pages, copyright pages, illustrations, spine shots, or condition images alongside the book record
  • Cover replacement — replace a cover from camera, upload, or online search; preview results in the lightbox before choosing one
AI Research Briefings
  • Generated automatically in the background as you add books — no manual action needed; briefings appear in real time
  • Small batches (≤ 25 books) generate immediately via a Firestore trigger; larger batches are queued for a scheduled function that runs every 2 hours
  • All briefings use Perplexity Sonar Pro with web-grounded verification and structured output
  • Supports three genre modes: fiction (spoiler-free by default — plot/characters/analysis/discussion questions), non-fiction (themes/key concepts/analysis/takeaways), and reference (editorial approach/contents overview/production notes/notable features/ideal-for)
  • The Regenerate button appears when a book’s existing briefing was generated by an older or less appropriate model, and also after metadata refresh changes title or author
  • Daily generation limit: 100 briefings per day, with admin override
Audio Briefings
  • Generate a narrated audio version of any briefing on demand
  • Gemini 2.5 Pro writes a natural-language narration script from the briefing content; Gemini 2.5 Pro TTS synthesizes it to speech and stores a WAV file in Firebase Storage
  • Long scripts are automatically split into chunks for synthesis and concatenated into a single WAV; duration is calculated from the actual WAV header
  • When Gemini 2.5 Pro TTS hits its daily rate limit, synthesis automatically falls back to Gemini 2.5 Flash TTS for the rest of the day. A notice appears when lower-quality audio was used, with a “Regenerate Higher Quality Audio” button once Pro TTS is available again
  • After queuing audio, the app polls for completion and updates the panel automatically when ready
  • Three voice options: Kore, Puck, Charon
  • Fiction titles produce separate safe and spoiler-inclusive audio variants; spoiler audio requires confirmation before generating
  • Audio metadata (voice, duration, status, TTS model used) is stored per book; the listen button appears automatically once audio is ready
Wikipedia Lookup
  • Available on every book’s detail page — one tap opens an in-app modal showing the article summary, thumbnail, and Wikidata description
  • Pass A — direct Wikipedia REST lookup verified against the book type and author name; fast, no AI cost
  • Pass B — if Pass A finds nothing, a Gemini Cloud Function identifies the exact Wikipedia article title for the book and author
  • Shows the book’s article when one exists; falls back to the author’s article with a notice; offers a “Search Wikipedia” link if neither is found
  • Pass B is opt-in when sharing — shelf owners choose whether viewers can trigger AI Wikipedia lookup
Shelf Sharing
  • Generate a public, read-only share link for any shelf — viewers need no account or sign-in
  • One active link per shelf at a time; any number of people can use the same link simultaneously
  • Per-share controls: include personal notes (off by default), enable AI-powered Wikipedia lookup for viewers, and enable audio briefings for viewers
  • Viewers can browse books, read briefings, view Wikipedia articles, and navigate the shelf — but cannot edit, add, or remove anything
  • Revoke links from the shelf’s Share button or from Account Settings at any time
  • All active share links are visible and manageable from Account Settings in one place
  • The shared detail panel has an expand toggle for a wider reading view; state persists
  • Cover lightbox automatically attempts a higher-resolution Google Books image before falling back to the original
Reading Tracker
  • Log reading status: Want to Read, Currently Reading, Read, Did Not Finish
  • Record start and finish dates, star ratings (1–5), and personal notes separate from publisher notes
  • Filter and sort your catalog by reading status at any time
Import & Export
  • Export — full backup ZIP (catalog + covers), JSON, or CSV
  • Import — ZIP, JSON, this app’s CSV, or a Goodreads export CSV
  • Goodreads and backup imports are automatically enriched from Open Library and Google Books when metadata or covers are available
Cloud & Account
  • Catalog and briefing cache are stored in Cloud Firestore, isolated per account, with real-time sync
  • Cover images and additional book photos are stored separately from the catalog data in Firebase Storage and per-book photo documents
  • Background-generated briefings appear automatically without a page reload
  • Guest Mode — full functionality without signing in; books are saved to Firestore under an anonymous account; sign in with Google at any time to permanently link the library to your account
  • Account Settings — export a full backup ZIP (catalog JSON + all cover images) or permanently delete your account and all associated data; the Backups section shows job history with download links for completed exports
  • Active shelf selection and sort preferences remembered across sessions
Design & Layout
  • Desktop layout — three-column layout with a collapsible Add panel and an expandable Briefing panel; states persist across sessions; compact UI mode hides helper text for a denser view
  • Mobile-first — bottom navigation bar on phones, hardware back-button support, and safe-area insets for iPhone home bar and other notched phones
  • Vintage design — parchment-toned palette with Playfair Display, EB Garamond, and Courier Prime typefaces
  • No installation required — runs in any modern browser; no app download, no extensions
