TomeShelf Shared Shelf

Your personal library, beautifully organized.

TomeShelf transforms your book collection into a living, searchable catalog — with AI-powered identification, named shelves, automatically generated research briefings, and shareable read-only links so you can show off your collection without giving anyone access to your account.

Features
📚
Instant Barcode Scanning

Point your camera at any ISBN barcode and TomeShelf instantly retrieves the title, author, cover art, and publication details — no typing required.

📷
AI Photo Identification

No barcode? Photograph the cover, title page, or copyright page and let AI identify the book — even for older or obscure editions.

📸
Bulk Load

Photograph an entire bookshelf and AI identifies every title at once. Or capture any text listing books — an article, reading list, or bibliography — and AI extracts every title mentioned.

📖
TomeShelves

Organise your catalog into named shelves — Reading List, Cookbooks, Borrowed Books, or whatever fits your life. Select multiple books at once to move or remove them in bulk.

🔎
Auto-Generated Briefings

Rich, college-level discussion guides appear automatically as you add books — themes, historical context, literary analysis, and discussion questions. Fiction includes a spoiler/safe toggle.

🌎
Wikipedia Lookup

One tap to view the Wikipedia article for any book or its author in a clean in-app modal. A two-pass strategy — direct REST lookup first, AI-assisted if needed — maximises coverage.

⭐
Reading Tracker

Log reading status, start and finish dates, personal notes, and star ratings. Track what you’ve read, what’s on your shelf, and what’s next.

🔗
Share Your Shelves

Generate a read-only link for any shelf — viewers need no account. Control whether your personal notes and AI Wikipedia lookups are included. Revoke at any time.

📄
Portable Data

Export to JSON or CSV; import from Goodreads exports or your own backups, with covers and metadata enriched automatically. Your data is always yours.

Everything TomeShelf can do.

Adding Books
  • Barcode scanning — point your camera at any ISBN barcode; title, author, cover art, and publication details fill in instantly (html5-qrcode fallback for older browsers)
  • AI Photo Identification — photograph up to 3 images of a cover, spine, title page, or copyright page; Gemini extracts metadata; Open Library and Google Books searched for a match; multiple candidates shown with thumbnails
  • Bulk Load: Books — photograph a whole bookshelf or stack; AI reads every visible spine and cover; review a ✓/✗ card list before anything is added; manual correction fallback for unmatched titles
  • Bulk Load: Titles in Text — photograph an article, reading list, bibliography, or any text mentioning books; AI extracts every title found
  • Cover Search — search Open Library and Google Books by title; results shown as a thumbnail grid; tap to preview full-size in a lightbox, then apply with one tap
Managing Your Library
  • TomeShelves — create and name as many shelves as you like; rename or delete at any time; last-viewed shelf restored on next open
  • Multi-book selection — select any or all books on a shelf; move or delete in bulk with confirmation
  • Full catalog fields — title, author, year, publisher, edition, ISBN, subjects, cover art, condition, shelf location, reading notes, and personal notes
  • Search, sort & filter — search by any field; filter by reading status, condition, or shelf; sort by title, author, year, rating, or recently added
  • Duplicate detection — adding a book with an identical title and author shows a warning; “Add Anyway” allows legitimate multiple editions
AI Research Briefings
  • Generated automatically in the background as you add books — no manual action needed; briefings appear in real time
  • Small batches (≤ 25 books) generate immediately; larger batches queue for a scheduled function running every 2 hours
  • Books published before 2024 → Google Gemini 2.5 Flash  |  Books from 2024 onward → Perplexity Sonar Pro (web-grounded, post-cutoff knowledge)
  • Fiction briefings include spoiler and spoiler-free versions of summaries, character notes, and discussion questions — toggle with one tap
  • A Regenerate button appears only when a book’s existing briefing was produced by an older or less appropriate model
Wikipedia Lookup
  • Available on every book’s detail page — one tap opens an in-app modal showing the article summary, thumbnail, and Wikidata description
  • Pass A — direct Wikipedia REST lookup verified against the book type and author name; fast, no AI cost
  • Pass B — if Pass A finds nothing, a Gemini Cloud Function identifies the exact Wikipedia article title for the book and for its author
  • Shows the book’s article when one exists; falls back to the author’s article with a notice; offers a “Search Wikipedia” link if neither is found
  • Pass B is opt-in when sharing — shelf owners choose whether viewers can trigger AI Wikipedia lookup
Shelf Sharing
  • Generate a public, read-only share link for any shelf — viewers need no account or sign-in
  • One active link per shelf at a time; any number of people can use the same link simultaneously
  • Per-share controls: include personal notes (off by default); enable AI-powered Wikipedia lookups for viewers
  • Viewers can browse books, read briefings, view Wikipedia articles, and navigate the shelf — but cannot edit, add, or remove anything
  • Revoke links from the shelf’s Share button or from Account Settings at any time
Reading Tracker
  • Log reading status: Want to Read, Currently Reading, Read, Did Not Finish
  • Record start and finish dates, star ratings (1–5), and personal notes separate from publisher notes
  • Filter and sort your catalog by reading status at any time
Import & Export
  • Export — full JSON backup or flat CSV, available from Account Settings
  • Import — JSON backup, this app’s own CSV format, or a Goodreads export CSV
  • Goodreads imports are automatically enriched — cover art and full metadata fetched from Open Library and Google Books
Cloud & Account
  • Entire catalog and briefing cache synced to Cloud Firestore per account, isolated per user, in real time
  • Background-generated briefings appear instantly without a page reload
  • Guest Mode — full functionality without signing in; books saved to an anonymous account; sign in with Google at any time to permanently keep your library
  • Account Settings — export a JSON backup, or permanently delete your account and all associated data
Design & Mobile
  • Vintage parchment aesthetic — warm creams, golds, and ink tones; Playfair Display headings, EB Garamond body, Courier Prime labels
  • Mobile-first — bottom navigation bar on phones, full three-column layout on desktop, hardware back-button support, safe-area insets for notched phones
  • No installation required — runs in any modern browser; no app download, no extensions
